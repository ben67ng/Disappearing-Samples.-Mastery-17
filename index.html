IELTS Memory Challenge

Sample IELTS Responses (5 minutes)

  1. What needs to be improved in public transport?

    To begin with, the most pressing issue is reliability, since buses and trains in many cities are frequently delayed, which undermines people’s confidence in the system. Moreover, comfort should not be overlooked; overcrowded vehicles make commuting both stressful and exhausting. What’s more, digital improvements such as real-time tracking apps and contactless payments could transform public transport into a far more convenient and appealing option. Ultimately, unless these aspects are addressed, many people will continue to rely on private vehicles.
  2. Will there be fewer cars if public transport is free?

    While it’s true that making public transport free might encourage certain groups, such as students or low-income workers, to switch, I don’t think it would significantly reduce car use overall. This is because convenience often outweighs cost: drivers can travel directly from door to door, whereas public transport still involves waiting and transferring. On balance, I believe that speed, coverage, and efficiency are far more decisive factors than price.
  3. Who likes to travel more, older people or younger people?

    It’s difficult to generalize, but I would argue that younger people tend to travel more frequently. One reason is that they usually have fewer family or financial responsibilities, which gives them greater freedom to explore. In addition, they often seek out adventurous experiences, such as backpacking or hiking, that appeal less to older generations. Having said that, older people also enjoy traveling, though they are more inclined to choose comfortable or culturally enriching trips, such as cruises or guided tours. In short, both groups like traveling, but they do so for rather different reasons.
  4. Will people travel abroad more often if air travel becomes cheaper?

    In my view, lower ticket prices would certainly lead to a rise in international travel, particularly among young adults and families who are currently restricted by cost. After all, many people already have the desire to see new places, and what holds them back is financial rather than motivational. However, it is worth pointing out that growing awareness of environmental issues might restrain this trend. So while cheaper air travel would no doubt increase demand, in the long run, governments may have to balance affordability with sustainability.
  5. What will become more popular in the future: air travel or train travel?

    That very much depends on the distance involved. For cross-continental journeys, air travel will almost certainly remain dominant, simply because it is so much faster. By contrast, within countries or across short international routes, high-speed trains are becoming increasingly popular, particularly in Europe and parts of Asia, as they are more environmentally friendly and take passengers directly from city center to city center. Personally, I expect trains to expand their share of the market, but it is hard to imagine a future where airplanes are not the preferred option for long-haul travel.
  6. What are the advantages and disadvantages of sea travel?

    On the positive side, sea travel can be uniquely relaxing, since passengers are able to enjoy the open scenery and benefit from a slower pace of life. In addition, cruise ships often provide a wide range of entertainment and facilities, which makes the journey itself part of the holiday. Nevertheless, there are clear drawbacks: it is considerably slower than air or rail travel, it can be highly weather-dependent, and for many routes it is simply impractical. Consequently, while sea travel does have its advantages, it is likely to remain a niche option, chosen more for leisure than for everyday transport.
